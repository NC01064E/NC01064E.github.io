About Nicholas Cerbone

Nicholas A. Cerbone is the Founder and Executive Director of the CivicOS Institute, a civic technology nonprofit dedicated to bridging the gap between citizens and their government through open-source tools, data transparency, and community engagement.

A Palm Beach County native with deep roots in public service, Nicholas spent seven years as a Deputy Clerk of the Court — witnessing firsthand how opaque systems disenfranchise ordinary citizens. That experience, combined with his work as an educator in the School District of Palm Beach County, shaped his conviction that civic participation should be accessible, understandable, and actionable for everyone.

Nicholas leads CivicOS with a hands-on approach — managing digital infrastructure, directing strategic partnerships, and personally building the open-source tools that power the Institute's mission. He is a published author (The Open Source Student), an advocate for technology literacy in education, and a firm believer that the best civic tech is built by the communities it serves.

He is the father of nine children and lives in Palm Beach County, Florida.

The CivicOS Mission

Open Government

Government data belongs to the public. CivicOS builds tools that make public records searchable, understandable, and actionable — from court filings to budget data to legislative tracking.

Community Power

Civic technology should be built with communities, not imposed on them. CivicOS centers community voices in the design and deployment of every tool and platform.

Open Source

All CivicOS software is open source. No paywalls, no vendor lock-in, no proprietary barriers. Public infrastructure demands public code.

Education & Literacy

Civic participation requires understanding. CivicOS develops educational resources and training programs that equip citizens with the knowledge to engage effectively.

What CivicOS Builds

Civic Data Platforms

Searchable, structured databases of public records — court data, legislation, budgets — built for citizens, journalists, and researchers.

Community Engagement Tools

Open-source platforms for public comment, civic dialogue, and participatory governance that put community voices at the center.

AI-Powered Civic Intelligence

Responsible AI tools that summarize legislation, track policy changes, and surface what matters to the communities most affected.

Founder's Story

I spent seven years inside the court system as a Deputy Clerk of the Court in Palm Beach County. Every day, I watched citizens navigate a system that was never designed to be navigable — complex filings, opaque procedures, and a presumption that you'd need a lawyer just to understand your own case.

Later, as a substitute teacher across Palm Beach County schools, I saw a different kind of gap: students and families who lacked the tools, knowledge, or access to participate meaningfully in the civic life of their communities.

CivicOS exists to close both gaps. We build open-source civic technology — because the tools of democracy should be free, transparent, and accountable to the people they serve.

Technology is only as good as the community it empowers. That's the standard we hold ourselves to.

— Nicholas A. Cerbone
