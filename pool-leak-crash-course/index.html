Six facts to memorize

Pools evaporate, but route calls need faster evidence. Pressure testing checks plumbing. Dye testing confirms visible leak points. Pump-on and pump-off clues matter. Shell and finish defects are different repair conversations. Good techs think like detectives.

References

Source Notes

This training is educational material, not a substitute for field training, licensing, electrical safety procedures, or approved service procedures. Pressure testing, diving, excavation, and pool-light work should be performed only by trained and authorized technicians.

  • Anderson Manufacturing: Pressure Test Plumbing

    Used for pressure testing as a process-of-elimination method for structure vs. plumbing leaks.

  • National Plasterers Council: Pool Plaster FAQ

    Used for generic plaster/finish concepts: cracking classes, bonding, delamination, spalls, chemistry, and repair judgment.

  • Pool Engineering: Minor Gunite or Shotcrete Repair

    Used for generic shell repair concepts: sounding, removing weak material, roughening, cleaning, and bonding to sound substrate.

  • NPT: Pool Finish Startup Procedure

    Used for startup concepts after new plaster: fill discipline, brushing, water balance, and the 28-day care window.

  • Electrical Safety Foundation International: Pool and Spa Safety

    Used for general electrical safety awareness around pools, including GFCI, grounding, and keeping outdoor receptacles dry.
